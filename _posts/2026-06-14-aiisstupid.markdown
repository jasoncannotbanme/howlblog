I've been using **claude** and **codex** and some other sources to make a hypixel bedwars botting script using mineflayer named hyphex. I realised how badly i depended on AI autocomplete and AI to make a roadmap and it telling me exactly what to do, because had it been 2-3 years ago i wouldn't have been able to make the project in the first place.

so here comes my **big question**, has AI made us stupid? or faster.

This was one of the topics discussed in my communications class. Albeit one of the worst classes ive attended, it was that one topic that actually caught my ear. Thank god.

Now i usually dont partake in "educational" conversation's that take place inside my class"es" but if you look at this from a wider perspective, AI has made our life's easier. I don't think any student in the whole world wouldve been able to achieve the backlog of literally thousands of sheets in the duration of their degree, as compared to well pre 2020, we usually ended up paying someone to finish our stuff, or take shortcuts which risked our GPA. 

Just last month, I had compulsary NGO work assigned to us, I did not care enough to actually do the stuff, make weekly logs and reports and submit it but i did NOT wanna risk my grade because i was "lazy". It wasn't me being lazy, i just had a bunch of other stuff in my life to take care off, NGO work to me sounded and still sounds useless because out of what? hundreds and thousands of students, only some pay actually attention to the work itself. So what do i do here? I run claude code, make a 20 liner prompt, click a few photos of me "doing the work" and let claude make a way out of my league PDF report of what i supposdly did, yikes!

My university realised the absolute stupidity of students to not even reference work they made using AI with actual sources. We as students have become way to procrastinated to actual make an effort towards the degree we chose to study, because we are too busy larping what our lifes could be instead of actually working towards it, including me.
